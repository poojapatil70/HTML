Tigers (Panthera tigris) are the world's largest cats, known for their distinctive orange fur with black stripes, unique to each individual like fingerprints, and are powerful, solitary apex predators found in Asian forests and grasslands, excellent swimmers, and endangered due to habitat loss, with major subspecies including the Bengal and Siberian (Amur) tigers. They hunt large prey like deer and boar using stealth, ambush tactics, and bursts of speed, relying on sight and hearing, though they are facing significant threats from humans.
